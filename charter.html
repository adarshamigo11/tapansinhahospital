Citizen’s Charter

Service standards, patients’ rights, grievance redressal and RTI information

Our Commitment to Citizens

This Citizen’s Charter outlines the services provided by Tapan Sinha Memorial Hospital and the standards patients and the public can expect, in line with the guidelines of the Department of Health & Family Welfare, Government of West Bengal.

Service Standards

ServiceStandard / Time Norm
OPD RegistrationToken issued within 15 minutes of arrival during OPD hours
Emergency TriageImmediate assessment on arrival, 24x7
Laboratory Report (Routine)Same day, before 4:00 PM
X-Ray / USG ReportSame day or next working day
Discharge SummaryProvided at the time of discharge
Grievance AcknowledgementWithin 3 working days
Grievance ResolutionWithin 15 working days

Patients’ Rights

  • Right to timely and dignified medical treatment
  • Right to information about diagnosis, treatment and cost
  • Right to informed consent before any procedure
  • Right to confidentiality of medical records
  • Right to seek a second opinion
  • Right to lodge a grievance without fear of denial of treatment

Patients’ Responsibilities

  • Provide accurate and complete health information to treating staff
  • Follow prescribed treatment and hospital protocols
  • Maintain cleanliness and avoid overcrowding in wards
  • Treat hospital staff and fellow patients with courtesy
  • Carry a valid identity document / Swasthya Sathi card for registration
